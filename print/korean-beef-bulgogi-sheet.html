Korean Beef Bulgogi Skewers

for 4!
Chef's secret
Enzymes in the grated pear actually break down the meat and make it melt-in-your-mouth tender. Don't skip the pear!

Ingredients For 4

  • 400g beef sirloin or scotch fillet (thinly sliced)
  • 1 ripe pear (grated)
  • 60ml soy sauce
  • 30g brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp sesame oil
  • 4 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger (grated)
  • 8 wooden skewers (soaked in water 20 min)

To serve

  • Butter lettuce leaves
  • Steamed jasmine rice
  • 1 cucumber (sliced)
  • Dipping sauce (1 tbsp honey + 1 tbsp soy + squeeze lime)

Directions

1
The Magic Marinade

Grate the pear into a bowl. Add soy sauce, brown sugar, sesame oil, minced garlic and ginger — mix well until the sugar dissolves.

2
Marinate the Beef

Add the thinly sliced beef to the marinade. Coat every piece well and leave to rest for 20–30 minutes so all those flavours soak in.

3
Fold and Skewer

Fold each slice back and forth like a ribbon, then push it onto a soaked wooden skewer. Pack the meat nice and close together.

4
Cook on High Heat

Get your pan very hot, then cook skewers 3–4 min each side until caramelised and charred at the edges. Don't crowd the pan!

5
Check It's Done

Slice through the thickest piece of beef — there should be no pink left. If you see pink, give it another minute.

6
Build the Wrap

Lay a lettuce leaf flat. Add a spoonful of rice, a few cucumber slices, then slide the beef off the skewer on top.

7
Dip and Eat

Spoon over the dipping sauce, fold the lettuce up and eat Korean BBQ style — messy and delicious!
